\begin{helper}
Fix natural numbers \(p,m\) and a Boolean rank-increase sequence
\[
  z:\{0,\ldots,m\}\to\{\mathrm{false},\mathrm{true}\}.
\]
Suppose \(z(m)=\mathrm{false}\), and let \(z'\) be the restricted sequence on
\(\{0,\ldots,m-1\}\). Then the number of bad tuples of length \(m+1\) with
fixed increase pattern \(z\), as counted by
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseCount}\), is at most
\[
  F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseCount(p,m,z')\cdot
  2^{\operatorname{wt}(z)}\,2^{p-\operatorname{wt}(z)}.
\]
Here \(\operatorname{wt}(z)\) is the number of true entries of \(z\), as in
\(\noderef{BinarySequenceWeight}\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let \(T\) be the set of bad length-\(m+1\) tuples with fixed increase pattern
\(z\), and let \(P\) be the set of bad length-\(m\) tuples with fixed increase
pattern \(z'\). The false-last hypothesis and
\(\noderef{BinarySequenceWeightSnoc}\) imply
\[
  \operatorname{wt}(z)=\operatorname{wt}(z').
\]

For any prefix \(pref\in P\), its final rank is \(\operatorname{wt}(z)\). If
\(m=0\), this follows from \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRankZero}\) and the preceding
weight identity. If \(m>0\), then
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleRankIncreaseSetCard}\) identifies the final rank of
\(pref\) with the number of rank-increase indices of \(pref\); the fixed
increase condition in the definition of \(P\) identifies those indices exactly
with the true entries of \(z'\), whose number is \(\operatorname{wt}(z')\).
Thus the final rank is \(\operatorname{wt}(z)\).

Map a tuple \(ab\in T\) to its prefix \(ab'\in P\), supplied by
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreasePrefixRestriction}\), together with its final
pair \((a_m,b_m)\). As in \(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseTrueLastBound}\),
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleRankIncreaseSetCard}\) shows that the final rank of
\(ab\) is \(\operatorname{wt}(z)\), and
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleRankSnocPrefixLast}\) shows that replacing the final
second coordinate by \(0\) does not change this rank. The bad-tuple condition
also gives
\[
  \langle a_j,b_m\rangle=1
  \quad\text{for every }0\le j\le m.
\]
Therefore, over each fixed prefix \(pref\in P\), the possible final pairs lie
in the span-restricted last-pair fiber counted by
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleLastPairSpanFiberBound}\) with
\(t=\operatorname{wt}(z)\). The prefix-rank verification above supplies that
node's hypothesis, and the node bounds the fiber by
\[
  2^{\operatorname{wt}(z)}\,2^{p-\operatorname{wt}(z)}.
\]

The map from \(T\) into the disjoint union over prefixes \(pref\in P\) of these
last-pair fibers is injective, because the prefix recovers the first \(m\)
pairs and the recorded final pair recovers the last pair. Hence
\[
  |T|
  \le \sum_{pref\in P}
      2^{\operatorname{wt}(z)}\,2^{p-\operatorname{wt}(z)}
  = |P|\,2^{\operatorname{wt}(z)}\,2^{p-\operatorname{wt}(z)}.
\]
This is the claimed inequality.
\end{proof}
