\begin{helper}
Let \(s,a\in\mathbb N\) and let \(\delta,\eta\in\mathbb R\).  Assume
\(s>0\), \(\eta\ge0\), \(\delta\ge0\), and \(a\le\delta s\).  Then
\[
  \left(\frac{s+a-1}{2}-\frac{a^2}{2s}\right)
    -\left(\frac52\delta+\eta\right)
  \le
  2s-3-
  \frac{\frac32s^2+as-\frac52s+\eta s}{s+a}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
All quantities in the displayed inequalities are interpreted as real numbers.
Since \(s>0\) and \(a\ge0\), we have \(s+a>0\).  A direct expansion gives
\[
\begin{aligned}
&2s-3-
  \frac{\frac32s^2+as-\frac52s+\eta s}{s+a}
  -\left(\frac{s+a-1}{2}-\frac{a^2}{2s}\right)\\
&\qquad =
  \frac{a^3}{2s(s+a)}
  -\frac{5a}{2(s+a)}
  -\frac{\eta s}{s+a}.
\end{aligned}
\]
The first term on the right is nonnegative.  Also \(a\le\delta s\) and
\(s>0\) imply \(a/s\le\delta\), while \(s+a\ge s\) gives
\[
  \frac{a}{s+a}\le \frac{a}{s}\le\delta.
\]
Therefore
\[
  -\frac{5a}{2(s+a)}\ge -\frac52\delta.
\]
Similarly, \(s/(s+a)\le1\) and \(\eta\ge0\), so
\[
  -\frac{\eta s}{s+a}\ge-\eta.
\]
Combining these three estimates gives
\[
  2s-3-
  \frac{\frac32s^2+as-\frac52s+\eta s}{s+a}
  -\left(\frac{s+a-1}{2}-\frac{a^2}{2s}\right)
  \ge -\frac52\delta-\eta,
\]
which is exactly the claimed inequality after moving the final loss term to
the left hand side.
\end{proof}
