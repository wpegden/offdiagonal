\begin{helper}
Let \(\ell,s,n\) be natural numbers with \(3\le\ell\).  If
\[
  n\le 2^{(s/2-4)(\ell-1)},
\]
then
\[
  n^s\cdot 2^{(-s^2/2+4s)(\ell-1)}\le 1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let
\[
  A=(s/2-4)(\ell-1)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  B=(-s^2/2+4s)(\ell-1).
\]
The hypothesis says \(n\le 2^A\).  Since \(n\ge0\), raising both sides to
the natural power \(s\) gives
\[
  n^s\le (2^A)^s=2^{As}.
\]
Multiplying by the nonnegative factor \(2^B\), we obtain
\[
  n^s 2^B\le 2^{As}2^B=2^{As+B}.
\]
Finally,
\[
  As+B
  =
  (s/2-4)(\ell-1)s+(-s^2/2+4s)(\ell-1)
  =0.
\]
Therefore \(2^{As+B}=2^0=1\), and the claimed inequality follows.
\end{proof}
