\begin{lemma}
For every \(\varepsilon>0\), there are \(\delta>0\) and \(s_0\) such that whenever \(s\ge s_0\) and \(a\ge 0\) is an integer satisfying \(a\le \delta s\), there exists a loopless \(T_s\)-free digraph \(D\) on
\[
  2^{2s-3}-2^{s-1}-2^{s-2}+1
\]
vertices such that
\[
  \overrightarrow{i_{s+a}}(D)\le
  2^{\frac32s^2+as-\frac52s+\varepsilon s}.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
SKETCH:
Start with the summand bound from \noderef{F2ForwardIndependentTuples}. Write \(t=s-j\). Then
\[
  \binom{k}{t}=\binom{s+a}{j+a}
\]
and the \(t\)-th summand is at most
\[
  \left(\frac{e(s+a)}{a+1}\right)^{j+a}
  2^{s^2/2+s(s+a)-3s/2-(s+a)+3j/2-j^2/2}.
\]
The exponent in \(j\) is maximized for \(j=O(\log s)\), and since \(a=o(s)\), the prefactor contributes only \(2^{o(s)}\). Thus every summand is at most \(2^{s^2/2+s(s+a)-3s/2-(s+a)+o(s)}\), and summing over at most \(s\) values of \(t\) is absorbed in the \(o(s)\) term.
\end{proof}
