\begin{helper}
For every \(\varepsilon>0\), there is \(s_0\) such that, for every
integer \(s\ge s_0\),
\[
  \frac12\left(\log_2(2es)+\frac32\right)^2
  \le \frac{\varepsilon s}{4}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Put \(C=2e\).  The standard estimate \((\log y)^r=o(y)\) as
\(y\to\infty\), applied with \(r=1\) and \(r=2\), gives
\[
  \frac{(\log(Cx))^2}{x}\to0,\qquad
  \frac{\log(Cx)}{x}\to0,\qquad
  \frac1x\to0
  \qquad (x\to\infty).
\]
Since \(\log_2(Cx)=\log(Cx)/\log 2\), expanding the square gives
\[
  \frac{\frac12(\log_2(Cx)+3/2)^2}{x}
  =
  \frac{1}{2(\log 2)^2}\frac{(\log(Cx))^2}{x}
  +\frac{3}{2\log 2}\frac{\log(Cx)}{x}
  +\frac98\frac1x,
\]
and therefore the left-hand side tends to \(0\) as \(x\to\infty\).
Thus, since \(\varepsilon/4>0\), the displayed ratio is \(<\varepsilon/4\)
for all sufficiently large \(x\).  Applying this to integer \(s\), and
choosing \(s_0\) at least \(1\), gives
\[
  \frac{\frac12(\log_2(2es)+3/2)^2}{s}<\frac{\varepsilon}{4}.
\]
Multiplying by the positive number \(s\) yields the desired inequality.
\end{proof}
