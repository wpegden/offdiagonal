\begin{helper}
Let \(C>1\).  For all sufficiently large natural numbers \(s\),
\[
  2^{(1-\frac{1}{2C})s}
  \le
  2^{2s-3}-2^{s-1}-2^{s-2}+1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{CloseToDiagonalVertexCountLowerBound}\) with
\(\rho=1/2\), for all sufficiently large \(s\),
\[
  \frac12\,2^{2s-3}
  \le
  2^{2s-3}-2^{s-1}-2^{s-2}+1. \tag{1}
\]
Increase the threshold so that \(s\ge8\).  Since \(C>1\), we have
\[
  1-\frac{1}{2C}\le 1.
\]
Therefore
\[
  (1-\frac{1}{2C})s\le s.
\]
Because the base \(2\) is at least \(1\), exponentiation is monotone in the
exponent, so
\[
  2^{(1-\frac{1}{2C})s}\le 2^s.
\]
For \(s\ge8\), we have \(s\le2s-4\), and hence
\[
  2^s\le2^{2s-4}=\frac12\,2^{2s-3}.
\]
Combining this inequality with (1) gives the desired bound.
\end{proof}
