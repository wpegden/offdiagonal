\begin{helper}
Let \(F\) be an \((n,d(F),\lambda(F))\)-loop graph on a finite vertex set.
Then the product-digraph vertex type
\[
  \{(a,b)\in V^2:ab\in E(F)\}
\]
from \(\noderef{ProductDigraphVertex}\) has cardinality \(d(F)n\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The product-digraph vertices can be counted by first choosing the first
coordinate.  More explicitly, \(\noderef{ProductDigraphVertex}\) identifies
the vertex type with the disjoint union, over \(a\in V\), of the fiber
\[
  \{b\in V:ab\in E(F)\}.
\]
For each fixed \(a\), this fiber has cardinality
\(\deg_F(a)\), which is the degree defined in \(\noderef{LoopGraphDegree}\).
Since \(F\) is an \((n,d(F),\lambda(F))\)-loop graph, the regularity clause in
\(\noderef{LoopGraphNdLambda}\) gives \(\deg_F(a)=d(F)\) for every \(a\), and
the vertex-count clause gives \(|V|=n\).  Therefore
\[
  \left|\{(a,b)\in V^2:ab\in E(F)\}\right|
  =\sum_{a\in V} d(F)
  =d(F)|V|
  =d(F)n.
\]
\end{proof}
