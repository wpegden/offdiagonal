\begin{helper}
For every \(2k\)-tuple
\[
  ab=(a_1,b_1,\ldots,a_k,b_k)\in(\mathbb F_2^p)^{2k}
\]
and every prefix parameter \(i\), the number of vectors
\[
  y\in\mathbb F_2^p
\]
such that
\[
  \langle a_j,y\rangle=1
  \quad\text{for every zero-based index }j<i
\]
is at most
\[
  2^{p-r_i},
\]
where \(r_i\) is the rank quantity from \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let \(V=\mathbb F_2^p\), and let \(S_i\le V\) be the span of the prefix
\(a\)-vectors with zero-based index \(j<i\). By \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\),
\(\dim S_i=r_i\). For each \(a\in V\), the map
\[
  \ell_a:V\to\mathbb F_2,\qquad \ell_a(y)=\langle a,y\rangle
\]
is linear in \(y\). With respect to the standard basis of \(V\), the assignment
\(a\mapsto \ell_a\) is a linear equivalence from \(V\) to its dual space: the
coordinate formula for \(\ell_a(y)\) is exactly the dot product. Hence the span
\(\Phi_i\) of the functionals \(\ell_{a_j}\), \(j<i\), has dimension
\[
  \dim \Phi_i=\dim S_i=r_i.
\]

Let
\[
  H_i=\{z\in V:\ell_{a_j}(z)=0\text{ for every }j<i\}.
\]
This is the dual coannihilator of \(\Phi_i\). Finite-dimensional duality gives
\[
  \dim H_i=p-\dim\Phi_i=p-r_i.
\]
Since \(H_i\) is a vector space over \(\mathbb F_2\), it has exactly
\(2^{p-r_i}\) elements.

It remains to compare the inhomogeneous solution set
\[
  Y_i=\{y\in V:\ell_{a_j}(y)=1\text{ for every }j<i\}
\]
with \(H_i\). If \(Y_i\) is empty, the desired bound is immediate. Otherwise
choose \(y_0\in Y_i\). For each \(y\in Y_i\), the difference \(y-y_0\) lies in
\(H_i\), because
\[
  \ell_{a_j}(y-y_0)=\ell_{a_j}(y)-\ell_{a_j}(y_0)=1-1=0
\]
for every \(j<i\). The map \(y\mapsto y-y_0\) is injective, so
\[
  |Y_i|\le |H_i|=2^{p-r_i}.
\]
\end{proof}
