\begin{helper}
For every \(p,k\) with \(0<k\), the number of bad \(2k\)-tuples over
\(\mathbb F_2^p\) is at most
\[
  \sum_{t=1}^p
    \binom{k}{t}\,
    2^{\,p(t+k)-\binom{t+1}{2}}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Partition the bad tuples according to their final rank
\[
  t=F2BadTupleRank(p,k,ab,k).
\]
For a bad tuple \(ab\), \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRankIncreaseSetCard}\) applies
because \(0<k\). It shows that this final rank lies in the interval
\(\{1,\ldots,p\}\). Therefore every bad tuple determines an element
\[
  t\in\{1,\ldots,p\}
\]
together with the same tuple \(ab\) in the fiber where the final rank equals
\(t\). The map from bad tuples to these tagged final-rank fibers is injective,
since the tuple itself is retained as part of the tagged data.

The cardinality of the disjoint union of the tagged final-rank fibers is the
sum of the cardinalities of the individual fibers. For each
\(t\in\{1,\ldots,p\}\), \(\noderef{F2BadTupleFinalRankFiberBound}\) bounds the
fiber of bad tuples of final rank \(t\) by
\[
  \binom{k}{t}\,
  2^{\,p(t+k)-\binom{t+1}{2}}.
\]
Summing these bounds over all \(t\in\{1,\ldots,p\}\) gives
\[
  \sum_{t=1}^p
    \binom{k}{t}\,
    2^{\,p(t+k)-\binom{t+1}{2}},
\]
which is the claimed upper bound for all bad tuples.
\end{proof}
