\begin{helper}
Let \(n,d_F,d_G\) be natural numbers with \(n>0\), \(d_F>0\), and \(d_G>0\),
and let
\(\lambda_F,\lambda_G,\eta\) be real numbers such that
\[
  \eta=
  \max\left\{
    \frac{\lambda_G^2}{d_G^2},
    \frac{\lambda_F\lambda_G}{d_Fd_G}
  \right\}.
\]
Suppose
\[
  \frac1{n^2}\le \frac{\lambda_G^2}{d_G^2},\qquad
  \frac{\lambda_G^2}{d_G^2}\le 1,\qquad
  \frac{\lambda_F\lambda_G}{d_Fd_G}\le 1.
\]
Then
\[
  \eta>0,\qquad \frac1{n^2}\le \eta,\qquad \eta\le 1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(n>0\), we have \(1/n^2>0\).  The first assumed component estimate and
the inequality
\[
  \frac{\lambda_G^2}{d_G^2}
  \le
  \max\left\{
    \frac{\lambda_G^2}{d_G^2},
    \frac{\lambda_F\lambda_G}{d_Fd_G}
  \right\}
  =\eta
\]
give \(1/n^2\le\eta\).  Combining this with \(1/n^2>0\) gives \(\eta>0\).

For the upper bound, both entries in the defining maximum for \(\eta\) are at
most \(1\) by hypothesis.  Therefore their maximum is at most \(1\), so
\(\eta\le1\).
\end{proof}
