\begin{helper}
Let \(G\) be an \((n,d,\lambda)\)-loop graph, and let \(T_G\) be the Euclidean
adjacency operator from \(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanOperator}\).  If
\(x\neq 0\) lies in the zero-sum subspace and \(T_Gx=\mu x\), then
\[
  |\mu|\le \lambda .
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{LoopGraphZeroSumEuclideanEigenpairNonprincipal}\), the scalar
\(\mu\) is a non-principal eigenvalue of \(G\).  The eigenvalue-bound clause in
\(\noderef{LoopGraphNdLambda}\) says that every non-principal eigenvalue has
absolute value at most \(\lambda\).  Applying that clause to \(\mu\) gives
\(|\mu|\le \lambda\).
\end{proof}
