\begin{helper}
Let \(D\) be a digraph on \(W\), let \(1\le\ell\), and let
\(\phi_c:[n]\to W\), for \(c<\ell-1\), be maps.  Suppose
\(S\subseteq[n]\) has \(|S|=s\) and every edge inside \(S\) receives the final
color \(\ell-1\) under \(\noderef{RandomHomomorphismColoring}\).  Then there
is a strictly increasing tuple \(v:[s]\to[n]\) whose entries all lie in \(S\)
such that, for every \(c<\ell-1\), the tuple
\[
  i\mapsto \phi_c(v_i)
\]
is forward independent in \(D\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(S\) is a finite subset of the linearly ordered set \([n]\) and
\(|S|=s\), list its elements in increasing order.  This gives a strictly
increasing tuple \(v:[s]\to[n]\), and each \(v_i\) lies in \(S\).

For \(i<j\), the two vertices \(v_i\) and \(v_j\) are distinct and both lie in
\(S\).  Hence the hypothesis on \(S\) says that the edge
\(\{v_i,v_j\}\) has final color \(\ell-1\).  Applying
\(\noderef{RandomHomomorphismFinalColorForwardIndependent}\) to this ordered
tuple, we conclude that for every \(c<\ell-1\) the tuple
\(i\mapsto \phi_c(v_i)\) is forward independent in \(D\), as required.
\end{proof}
