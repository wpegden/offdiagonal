\begin{helper}
Let \(C>1\).  For all sufficiently large natural numbers \(s\), set
\[
  k=\lceil Cs\rceil,\qquad
  E=sk+\frac{s^2}{2},\qquad
  p_0=\frac{k}{e}\,2^{-E/k}.
\]
Then
\[
  2^{(1-\frac{1}{2C})s}
  \le
  p_0\bigl(2^{2s-3}-2^{s-1}-2^{s-2}+1\bigr)-1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{CloseToDiagonalVertexCountLowerBound}\) with
\(\rho=1/2\), for all sufficiently large \(s\),
\[
  \frac12\,2^{2s-3}
  \le
  N:=2^{2s-3}-2^{s-1}-2^{s-2}+1. \tag{1}
\]
Increase the threshold for \(s\) so that
\[
  \frac{s}{16e}\ge2. \tag{2}
\]
This is possible because \(s/(16e)\to\infty\).

Since \(k=\lceil Cs\rceil\), we have \(k\ge Cs\), and since \(C>1\) this also
gives \(k\ge s\).  Hence
\[
  \frac{s^2}{2k}\le\frac{s}{2C}.
\]
Therefore
\[
\begin{aligned}
  2s-4-\frac{E}{k}
  &=2s-4-\frac{sk+s^2/2}{k}  \\
  &=s-4-\frac{s^2}{2k} \\
  &\ge \left(1-\frac{1}{2C}\right)s-4.
\end{aligned}
\]
Using monotonicity of \(2^x\) in the exponent and \(k\ge s\), we get
\[
\begin{aligned}
  p_0\cdot\frac12\,2^{2s-3}
  &=
  \frac{k}{e}\,2^{2s-4-E/k} \\
  &\ge
  \frac{s}{e}\,2^{(1-\frac{1}{2C})s-4} \\
  &=
  \frac{s}{16e}\,2^{(1-\frac{1}{2C})s}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Combining this with (1) and (2) gives
\[
  p_0N\ge2\cdot2^{(1-\frac{1}{2C})s}. \tag{3}
\]
The exponent \((1-\frac{1}{2C})s\) is nonnegative for \(s\ge0\), so
\[
  1\le2^{(1-\frac{1}{2C})s}.
\]
Thus (3) implies
\[
  2^{(1-\frac{1}{2C})s}
  \le p_0N-1,
\]
which is the desired inequality.
\end{proof}
