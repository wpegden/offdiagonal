\begin{helper}
Let \(V\) be a finite nonempty set and let \(A\subseteq V\). Then
\[
  \sum_{v\in V}\left(1_A(v)-\frac{|A|}{|V|}\right)^2\le |A|.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Write \(N=|V|\) and \(a=|A|\). Since \(V\) is nonempty, \(N>0\). Let \(c=a/N\). The ordinary indicator satisfies
\[
  \sum_{v\in V}1_A(v)=a
\]
and, because \(1_A(v)\) is always \(0\) or \(1\),
\[
  \sum_{v\in V}1_A(v)^2=a.
\]
Expanding the square and summing gives
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \sum_{v\in V}(1_A(v)-c)^2
  &= \sum_{v\in V}1_A(v)^2
     -2c\sum_{v\in V}1_A(v)
     +\sum_{v\in V}c^2  \\
  &= a-2c a+Nc^2.
\end{aligned}
\]
Substituting \(c=a/N\) yields
\[
  a-2\frac aN a+N\left(\frac aN\right)^2
  =
  a-\frac{a^2}{N}.
\]
The term \(a^2/N\) is nonnegative because \(N>0\). Hence
\[
  \sum_{v\in V}\left(1_A(v)-\frac{|A|}{|V|}\right)^2
  =a-\frac{a^2}{N}\le a=|A|.
\]
\end{proof}
