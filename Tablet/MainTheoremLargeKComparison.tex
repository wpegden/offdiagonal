\begin{helper}
Fix \(s\ge4\), \(k\ge2\), and real numbers \(A,B,Q,\eta\) with
\(B>0\), \(Q>0\), and \(\eta>0\).  Suppose
\[
  \eta\le \frac{B}{Q},
\]
\[
  A\frac{k^{s-3}}{(\log k)^{2s-6}}\le Q,
\]
and
\[
  1\le
  \frac{A}{200B}\frac{k^{s-2}}{(\log k)^{2s-6}}.
\]
Then
\[
  \frac{A}{200B}\frac{k^{s-2}}{(\log k)^{2s-6}}
  \le \frac{k}{100\eta}-1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(k\ge2\), we have \(k>0\) and \(\log k>0\).  From
\(\eta\le B/Q\) and \(Q>0\), multiplication by \(Q\) gives
\[
  \eta Q\le B.
\]
Because \(B>0\) and \(\eta>0\), this is equivalent to
\[
  \frac{Q}{B}\le \frac1\eta.
\]
Multiplying by \(k/100\ge0\), we obtain
\[
  \frac{kQ}{100B}\le \frac{k}{100\eta}. \tag{1}
\]

The assumed lower bound for \(Q\), multiplied by \(k/(100B)\ge0\), gives
\[
  \frac{k}{100B}\cdot
  A\frac{k^{s-3}}{(\log k)^{2s-6}}
  \le \frac{kQ}{100B}.
\]
Since \(s\ge4\), \(s-2=(s-3)+1\), so \(k^{s-2}=k\,k^{s-3}\).  Therefore
\[
  \frac{A}{100B}\frac{k^{s-2}}{(\log k)^{2s-6}}
  \le \frac{kQ}{100B}. \tag{2}
\]
Combining (1) and (2) yields
\[
  \frac{A}{100B}\frac{k^{s-2}}{(\log k)^{2s-6}}
  \le \frac{k}{100\eta}. \tag{3}
\]

Let
\[
  X=\frac{A}{200B}\frac{k^{s-2}}{(\log k)^{2s-6}}.
\]
The third hypothesis says \(1\le X\), while the left side of (3) is \(2X\).
Hence \(X\le 2X-1\).  Using (3), we have
\[
  X\le 2X-1
  \le \frac{k}{100\eta}-1,
\]
which is the desired inequality.
\end{proof}
