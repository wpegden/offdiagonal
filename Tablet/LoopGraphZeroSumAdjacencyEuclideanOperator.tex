\begin{definition}
Let \(G\) be an \((n,d,\lambda)\)-loop graph.  The zero-sum Euclidean adjacency
operator is the restriction of the Euclidean adjacency operator
\(T_G\) from \(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanOperator}\) to the zero-sum
subspace \(W_0(V(G))\) from \(\noderef{LoopGraphEuclideanZeroSumSubmodule}\).
The restriction is well-defined because
\(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanOperatorMapsZeroSum}\) shows that
\(T_G\) preserves \(W_0(V(G))\).
\end{definition}
