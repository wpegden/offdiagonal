\begin{helper}
Let \(D\) be a digraph on \(W\), let \(1\le\ell\), and let
\(\phi_c:[n]\to W\), for \(c<\ell-1\), be maps.  If
\[
  v:[s]\to[n]
\]
is strictly increasing and every edge \(\{v_i,v_j\}\), \(i<j\), receives the
final color \(\ell-1\) under \(\noderef{RandomHomomorphismColoring}\), then
for every \(c<\ell-1\) the tuple
\[
  i\mapsto \phi_c(v_i)
\]
is forward independent in \(D\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Fix \(c<\ell-1\).  By the definition of
\(\noderef{ForwardIndependentTuple}\), we must prove that for every \(i<j\)
there is no arc from \(\phi_c(v_i)\) to \(\phi_c(v_j)\).  Suppose such an arc
exists.
Because \(v\) is strictly increasing, the ordered endpoints of the unordered
edge \(\{v_i,v_j\}\) are \(v_i\le v_j\).  In the definition of
\(\noderef{RandomHomomorphismColoring}\), the existence of the arc
\[
  D(\phi_c(v_i),\phi_c(v_j))
\]
therefore makes \(c\) one of the first colors eligible for that edge.  The
coloring chooses the least eligible first color, so the edge must receive
some color \(m<\ell-1\).  This contradicts the hypothesis that the same edge
has final color \(\ell-1\).  Hence no such arc exists, and the tuple is
forward independent.
\end{proof}
