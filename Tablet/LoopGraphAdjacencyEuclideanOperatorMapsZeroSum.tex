\begin{helper}
Let \(G\) be an \((n,d,\lambda)\)-loop graph.  The Euclidean adjacency operator
\(T_G\) from \(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanOperator}\) maps the
zero-sum subspace \(W_0(V(G))\) from \(\noderef{LoopGraphEuclideanZeroSumSubmodule}\)
into itself.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let \(x\in W_0(V(G))\).  By the definition of \(W_0(V(G))\) in
\(\noderef{LoopGraphEuclideanZeroSumSubmodule}\), the coordinate sum of \(x\)
is zero.  The operator \(T_G\) has the same coordinates as the adjacency action
by \(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanOperator}\).  Applying
\(\noderef{LoopGraphNdLambdaAdjacencyActionZeroSum}\) to the coordinate function
of \(x\) therefore gives
\[
  \sum_{v\in V(G)} (T_Gx)(v)=0.
\]
By the defining condition of \(W_0(V(G))\), this says \(T_Gx\in W_0(V(G))\).
\end{proof}
