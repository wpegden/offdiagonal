\begin{helper}
Let \(G\) be a symmetric loop graph.  The Euclidean adjacency operator
\(T_G\) from \(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanOperator}\) is symmetric:
\[
  \langle T_Gx,y\rangle_{\ell^2(V)}
  =
  \langle x,T_Gy\rangle_{\ell^2(V)}
\]
for all \(x,y\in\ell^2(V)\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanInner}\), the left hand side is
\[
  \sum_{v\in V} (A_Gx)(v)y(v).
\]
The finite-sum self-adjointness identity
\(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyActionSelfAdjoint}\), applied using the symmetry of
\(G\), rewrites this as
\[
  \sum_{v\in V} x(v)(A_Gy)(v).
\]
Applying \(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanInner}\) again, now in the
reverse direction, identifies this last finite sum with
\(\langle x,T_Gy\rangle_{\ell^2(V)}\).  Hence \(T_G\) is symmetric.
\end{proof}
