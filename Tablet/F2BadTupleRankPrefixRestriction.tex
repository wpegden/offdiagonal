\begin{helper}
Let \(p,m\) be natural numbers, and let
\[
  ab:\{0,\ldots,m\}\to \mathbb F_2^p\times\mathbb F_2^p.
\]
Let \(ab'\) be the prefix of length \(m\), defined by \(ab'(j)=ab(j)\) for
zero-based \(j<m\). For every natural number \(i\le m\), the rank of the first
\(i\) vectors computed from \(ab\) is equal to the rank of the first \(i\)
vectors computed from \(ab'\):
\[
  \mathrm{F2BadTupleRank}(p,m+1,ab,i)
  =
  \mathrm{F2BadTupleRank}(p,m,ab',i).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\), the left-hand side is the dimension of the
span of all first coordinates \(a_{\ell+1}\) whose zero-based index
\(\ell\) satisfies \(\ell<i\) inside the length \(m+1\) tuple. Because
\(i\le m\), every such \(\ell\) also satisfies \(\ell<m\), so it is an index of
the prefix tuple \(ab'\). Conversely, every zero-based prefix index
\(\ell<m\) with \(\ell<i\) is also an index of the original length \(m+1\)
tuple, and \(ab'(\ell)=ab(\ell)\). Thus the two generating sets of vectors are
identical. Their spans over \(\mathbb F_2\) are therefore identical, and equal
subspaces have equal dimensions. This is the claimed rank equality.
\end{proof}
