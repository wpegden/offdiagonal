\begin{theorem}
Let \(s\to\infty\) and let \(a\) be a nonnegative integer such that \(a=o(s)\). Then
\[
  r(s,s+a)\ge (1+o(1))\frac{s}{e}\cdot
  2^{(s+a-1)/2-a^2/(2s)}.
\]
Equivalently, the formal statement uses the usual uniform \(\varepsilon\)-form for the \(1+o(1)\) lower factor when \(a/s\) is sufficiently small.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
SKETCH:
Set \(k=s+a\). By \noderef{F2ForwardIndependentNearDiagonalBound}, there is a \(T_s\)-free digraph \(D\) on
\[
  N=2^{2s-3}-2^{s-1}-2^{s-2}+1=(1+o(1))2^{2s-3}
\]
vertices with
\[
  \overrightarrow{i_k}(D)\le 2^{3s^2/2+as-5s/2+o(s)}.
\]
Use \noderef{DigraphToGraphIndependentSetBound} to obtain a \(K_s\)-free graph \(G\) with \(i_k(G)\le(e/k)^k\overrightarrow{i_k}(D)\). If \(i_k(G)=0\), then \(r(s,k)\ge N\), which is stronger. Otherwise, \noderef{SamplingKsFreeRamseyBound} with \(p=i_k(G)^{-1/k}\) gives
\[
  r(s,s+a)\ge (1+o(1))2^{2s-3}\frac{s}{e}
  2^{(-3s^2/2-as+5s/2+o(s))/(s+a)}.
\]
Expanding the exponent gives the displayed lower bound.
\end{proof}
