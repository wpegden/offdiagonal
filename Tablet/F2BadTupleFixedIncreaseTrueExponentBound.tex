\begin{helper}
Let \(p,m,u\) be natural numbers with \(u+1\le p\). Then
\[
2^{p(u+m)-\binom{u+1}{2}}\,
  \bigl(2^p2^{p-(u+1)}\bigr)
\le
2^{p((u+1)+(m+1))-\binom{(u+1)+1}{2}}.
\]
This is the exponent recurrence for a fixed rank-increase sequence whose last
bit is true, where \(u\) is the prefix weight.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Combining powers of \(2\), the left-hand side has exponent
\[
  p(u+m)-\binom{u+1}{2}+p+\bigl(p-(u+1)\bigr).
\]
The assumption \(u+1\le p\) makes the last subtraction ordinary. As in
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseFalseExponentBound}\), we have
\(\binom{u+1}{2}\le p(u+m)\), so the displayed exponent is obtained by ordinary
subtraction from \(p(u+m)\). Also,
\[
  \binom{(u+1)+1}{2}=\binom{u+1}{2}+(u+1),
\]
and
\[
  p((u+1)+(m+1))=p(u+m)+2p.
\]
Substituting these two identities shows that the left-hand exponent is exactly
the right-hand exponent. Hence the stated inequality of powers of \(2\) follows.
\end{proof}
