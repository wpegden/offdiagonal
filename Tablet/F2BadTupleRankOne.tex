\begin{helper}
Let \(ab=(a_1,b_1,\ldots,a_k,b_k)\) be a bad \(2k\)-tuple in the sense of
\(\noderef{F2BadTuple}\), and assume \(0<k\). Then the rank quantity from
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\) satisfies
\[
  r_1=1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(0<k\), the first pair \((a_1,b_1)\) exists. The bad-tuple condition from
\(\noderef{F2BadTuple}\), applied with the first index in both positions, gives
\[
  \langle a_1,b_1\rangle=1.
\]
Therefore \(a_1\ne0\), because the zero vector has dot product \(0\) with every
vector. By \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\), \(r_1\) is the dimension of the span of
the \(a\)-vectors with Lean index less than \(1\), which is exactly the singleton
set \(\{a_1\}\). The span of a single nonzero vector over \(\mathbb F_2\) is
one-dimensional. Hence \(r_1=1\).
\end{proof}
