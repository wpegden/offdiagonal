\begin{definition}
Let \(K\) be a field and \(t\ge 0\). The polarity graph \(G(t,K)\) has as vertices the points of the projective space \(\mathbb P(K^{t+1})\). Two, not necessarily distinct, projective points represented by nonzero vectors \(x\) and \(y\) are adjacent exactly when their dot product is zero.
\end{definition}
