\begin{helper}
For every \(2k\)-tuple
\[
  ab=(a_1,b_1,\ldots,a_k,b_k)\in(\mathbb F_2^p)^{2k}
\]
and every prefix length \(i\), let
\[
  U_i=\operatorname{span}\{a_j: j<i\}.
\]
If \(r_i\) is the rank quantity from \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\), then
\[
  |U_i|=2^{r_i}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\), \(r_i\) is the dimension of the subspace
\(U_i\) over \(\mathbb F_2\). A finite-dimensional vector space of dimension
\(d\) over \(\mathbb F_2\) has exactly \(2^d\) elements: choosing coordinates
with respect to any basis identifies the space with \((\mathbb F_2)^d\), and
each coordinate has two possible values. Applying this with \(d=r_i\) gives
\(|U_i|=2^{r_i}\).
\end{proof}
