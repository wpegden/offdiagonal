\begin{helper}
For every \(2k\)-tuple
\[
  ab=(a_1,b_1,\ldots,a_k,b_k)\in(\mathbb F_2^p)^{2k}
\]
and every prefix length \(i\), use the following zero-based Lean indexing
convention: the Lean index \(\ell\in\{0,\ldots,k-1\}\) refers to the paper
vector \(a_{\ell+1}\). Let
\[
  U_i=\operatorname{span}\{a_{\ell+1}: 0\le \ell<i\}.
\]
Thus \(U_i\) is the span of the first \(i\) paper \(a\)-vectors. If \(r_i\) is
the rank quantity from \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\), then
\[
  |U_i|=2^{r_i}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\), \(r_i\) is the dimension of the subspace
\[
  \operatorname{span}\{a_{\ell+1}:0\le \ell<i\}=U_i
\]
over \(\mathbb F_2\). A finite-dimensional vector space of dimension \(d\) over
\(\mathbb F_2\) has exactly \(2^d\) elements: choosing coordinates with respect
to any basis identifies the space with \((\mathbb F_2)^d\), and each coordinate
has two possible values. Applying this with \(d=r_i\) gives \(|U_i|=2^{r_i}\).
\end{proof}
