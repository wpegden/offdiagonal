\begin{helper}
For every \(2k\)-tuple
\[
  ab=(a_1,b_1,\ldots,a_k,b_k)\in(\mathbb F_2^p)^{2k}
\]
and every zero-based Lean index \(i<k\), the Lean vector at index \(i\) is the
paper vector \(a_{i+1}\). Let \(r_i\) be the rank quantity from
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\), and let
\[
  U_i=\operatorname{span}\{a_{\ell+1}:0\le \ell<i\}.
\]
Thus \(U_i\) is the span of the first \(i\) paper \(a\)-vectors. If
\(r_{i+1}=r_i\), then \(a_{i+1}\in U_i\), and
\[
  |U_i|\cdot
  \left|\{\,y\in\mathbb F_2^p:\langle a_{\ell+1},y\rangle=1
      \text{ for every zero-based }\ell<i+1\,\}\right|
  \le
  2^{r_i}\,2^{p-r_i}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The span defining \(r_{i+1}\) is obtained from the span defining \(r_i\) by
adjoining the single paper vector \(a_{i+1}\), because Lean index \(i\)
corresponds to paper index \(i+1\). If \(a_{i+1}\notin U_i\), adjoining
\(a_{i+1}\) adds one independent direction, so the dimension would increase by
one: \(r_{i+1}=r_i+1\). This contradicts the hypothesis \(r_{i+1}=r_i\). Hence
\(a_{i+1}\in U_i\).

By \(\noderef{F2BadTuplePrefixSpanCard}\), the span \(U_i\) has cardinality
\(2^{r_i}\). By \(\noderef{F2BadTuplePrefixFiberBound}\), applied to the prefix
of length \(i+1\), the number of vectors \(y\) satisfying
\(\langle a_{\ell+1},y\rangle=1\) for every zero-based \(\ell<i+1\), equivalently
for the paper vectors \(a_1,\ldots,a_{i+1}\), is at most \(2^{p-r_{i+1}}\).
Since \(r_{i+1}=r_i\), this upper bound is \(2^{p-r_i}\). Multiplying the two
bounds gives the stated product estimate.
\end{proof}
