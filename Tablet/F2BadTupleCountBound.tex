\begin{helper}
For every \(p,k\) with \(0<k\), the number of bad \(2k\)-tuples over
\(\mathbb F_2^p\), viewed as a real number, is at most
\[
  \sum_{t=1}^p
    \binom{k}{t}\,
    \operatorname{Real.rpow}\!\left(2,\,
      p(t+k)-\binom{t+1}{2}\right).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The natural-number estimate \(\noderef{F2BadTupleCountBoundNat}\) gives
\[
  \left|\{ab:F2BadTuple(p,k,ab)\}\right|
  \le
  \sum_{t=1}^p
    \binom{k}{t}\,
    2^{\,p(t+k)-\binom{t+1}{2}}.
\]
Cast this inequality from \(\mathbb N\) to \(\mathbb R\). The natural-number
cast preserves order, sums, products, and natural powers, so the right-hand
side becomes
\[
  \sum_{t=1}^p
    \bigl(\binom{k}{t}:\mathbb R\bigr)\,
    (2:\mathbb R)^{\,p(t+k)-\binom{t+1}{2}}.
\]
For each natural exponent \(n\), the real identity
\[
  (2:\mathbb R)^n=\operatorname{Real.rpow}(2,n)
\]
rewrites the ordinary real power as the real-power expression used downstream.
Applying this identity term by term in the finite sum gives exactly the stated
real-valued bound.
\end{proof}
