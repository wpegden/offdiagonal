\begin{helper}
Fix an integer \(s\ge4\).  There are an integer \(k_0\) and real constants
\(A,B,M\) such that
\[
  A>0,\qquad B>0,\qquad M>0,\qquad A\ge1,\qquad B\ge1,
\]
\[
  100(s+1)^3\le A,\qquad s+1\le M,\qquad
  B^{s+1}(s+1)^2\le M,\qquad 200B^{s+1}\le M.
\]
Moreover, for every integer \(k\ge k_0\), there is an integer \(m\ge1\).
If \(q=2^m\), then the concrete Galois field
\(\mathrm{GaloisField}(2,m)\) has \(q\) elements and
\[
  \frac{k}{B(\log k)^2}\le q\le \frac{k}{A(\log k)^2}.
\]
In addition,
\[
  \log q\le \log k,\qquad \log q>0,\qquad
  1\le \log k,\qquad M(\log k)^{2s+2}\le k.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Put
\[
  A=10^6(s+1)^4,\qquad B=2A,\qquad
  M=10^6B^{s+1}(s+1)^{2s+2}.
\]
Since \(s+1\ge1\), these constants are positive, \(A\ge1\), and \(B\ge1\).
The inequality \(100(s+1)^3\le A\) follows from \(s+1\ge1\) and
\(100\le10^6\).  Also \(s+1\le M\), because \(B^{s+1}\ge1\) and
\((s+1)^{2s+2}\ge s+1\).  Similarly,
\[
  B^{s+1}(s+1)^2\le M
\]
because \((s+1)^{2s+2}\ge (s+1)^2\), and
\[
  200B^{s+1}\le M
\]
because \((s+1)^{2s+2}\ge1\) and \(200\le10^6\).

The analytic input is that every fixed power of \(\log x\) is
\(o(x)\) as \(x\to\infty\).  Hence, after increasing \(k_0\) if necessary,
for every integer \(k\ge k_0\) we have
\[
  2\le \frac{k}{A(\log k)^2},\qquad
  1\le\log k,\qquad
  M(\log k)^{2s+2}\le k.
\]
Fix such a \(k\), and set
\[
  x=\frac{k}{A(\log k)^2}.
\]
Then \(x\ge1\).  Choose \(m\ge0\) with
\[
  2^m\le x<2^{m+1}.
\]
The stronger bound \(x\ge2\) rules out \(m=0\), so \(m\ge1\).  Let
\(q=2^m\).  The displayed dyadic inequalities give \(q\le x\) and
\(x/2<q\), hence \(x/2\le q\).  Since \(B=2A\), this is exactly
\[
  \frac{k}{B(\log k)^2}\le q\le \frac{k}{A(\log k)^2}.
\]

Because \(A\ge1\) and \(1\le\log k\), we have
\[
  A(\log k)^2\ge1,
\]
and hence \(q\le k/(A(\log k)^2)\le k\).  Monotonicity of the logarithm on
positive reals gives \(\log q\le\log k\).  Since \(m\ge1\), \(q=2^m\ge2>1\),
so \(\log q>0\).

Finally, \(m\ne0\), so the standard cardinality theorem for the concrete
Galois field gives
\[
  |\mathrm{GaloisField}(2,m)|=2^m=q.
\]
The inequalities \(1\le\log k\) and \(M(\log k)^{2s+2}\le k\) are exactly
the threshold conditions imposed above.
\end{proof}
