\begin{helper}
Fix an integer \(s\ge4\).  For all sufficiently large \(k\), there is an
integer \(m\ge1\).  If \(q=2^m\), then the Galois field
\(\mathbb F_{2^m}\) has \(q\) elements and
\[
  \frac{k}{2(\log k)^2}\le q\le \frac{k}{(\log k)^2}.
\]
Moreover \(\log q\le \log k\) and \(\log q>0\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The only analytic input is that \((\log x)^2=o(x)\) as \(x\to\infty\).
Therefore, after increasing the lower threshold for \(k\), we have both
\[
  (\log k)^2\le \frac{k}{2}
  \qquad\text{and}\qquad
  1\le \log k .
\]
The first inequality and positivity of \((\log k)^2\) imply
\[
  2\le \frac{k}{(\log k)^2}.
\]
Put \(x=k/(\log k)^2\).  Since \(x\ge1\), the Archimedean discreteness of
successive powers of \(2\) gives an integer \(m\ge0\) with
\[
  2^m\le x<2^{m+1}.
\]
The stronger bound \(x\ge2\) rules out \(m=0\), so \(m\ge1\).  With
\(q=2^m\), the displayed inequalities give \(q\le x\) and
\[
  \frac{x}{2}<q,
\]
hence in particular \(x/2\le q\).  Substituting \(x=k/(\log k)^2\) gives
\[
  \frac{k}{2(\log k)^2}\le q\le \frac{k}{(\log k)^2}.
\]
Since \(1\le\log k\), we have \(1\le(\log k)^2\), and therefore
\[
  q\le \frac{k}{(\log k)^2}\le k.
\]
The real logarithm is monotone on positive reals, so \(\log q\le\log k\).
Also \(m\ge1\), hence \(q=2^m\ge2>1\), and so \(\log q>0\).

Finally, the field used later is the concrete Galois field
\(\mathbb F_{2^m}\), formalized as \(\mathrm{GaloisField}(2,m)\).  Since
\(m\ne0\), the standard cardinality theorem for Galois fields gives
\(|\mathbb F_{2^m}|=2^m=q\).
\end{proof}
