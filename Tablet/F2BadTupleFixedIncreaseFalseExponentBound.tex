\begin{helper}
Let \(p,m,u\) be natural numbers with \(u\le p\). Then
\[
2^{p(u+m)-\binom{u+1}{2}}\,
  \bigl(2^u2^{p-u}\bigr)
\le
2^{p(u+m+1)-\binom{u+1}{2}}.
\]
This is the exponent recurrence for a fixed rank-increase sequence whose last
bit is false.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(u\le p\), the product \(2^u2^{p-u}\) is \(2^p\). It remains to compare
the exponents. The binomial term \(\binom{u+1}{2}\) is at most \(pu\), because
\[
  \binom{u+1}{2}=\frac{(u+1)u}{2}\le pu
\]
under \(u\le p\); hence it is also at most \(p(u+m)\). Therefore
\[
  \bigl(p(u+m)-\binom{u+1}{2}\bigr)+p
  = p(u+m+1)-\binom{u+1}{2}.
\]
Thus multiplying the previous bound by the false-last factor increases the
power of \(2\) by exactly \(p\), giving the stated inequality.
\end{proof}
