\begin{helper}
For every \(p,k\) and every Boolean rank-increase sequence
\[
  z:\{0,\ldots,k-1\}\to\{\mathrm{false},\mathrm{true}\}
\]
with \(\operatorname{wt}(z)\le p\), the number of bad \(2k\)-tuples with fixed
increase pattern \(z\) satisfies
\[
  F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseCount(p,k,z)
  \le
  2^{\,p(\operatorname{wt}(z)+k)-\binom{\operatorname{wt}(z)+1}{2}}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
We prove the claim by induction on \(k\). For \(k=0\), there is only the empty
tuple and the empty Boolean sequence. The weight is \(0\), the binomial term is
\(\binom{1}{2}=0\), and the right-hand side is \(2^0=1\), so the bound is
immediate from \(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseCount}\).

Assume the claim has been proved for length \(m\), and let
\[
  z:\{0,\ldots,m\}\to\{\mathrm{false},\mathrm{true}\}.
\]
Let \(z'\) be the restricted sequence \(z'(i)=z(i)\) for \(0\le i<m\). We
split according to the last bit \(z(m)\).

First suppose \(z(m)=\mathrm{true}\). Then
\(\noderef{BinarySequenceWeightSnoc}\) gives
\[
  \operatorname{wt}(z)=\operatorname{wt}(z')+1.
\]
Since \(\operatorname{wt}(z)\le p\), the induction hypothesis applies to
\(z'\). Combining it with the one-step true-last bound
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseTrueLastBound}\) gives
\[
  F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseCount(p,m+1,z)
  \le
  2^{p(\operatorname{wt}(z')+m)-\binom{\operatorname{wt}(z')+1}{2}}
  \cdot 2^p 2^{p-\operatorname{wt}(z)}.
\]
With \(u=\operatorname{wt}(z')\), this is exactly the exponent recurrence
proved in \(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseTrueExponentBound}\), so it is at
most
\[
  2^{p(\operatorname{wt}(z)+m+1)-\binom{\operatorname{wt}(z)+1}{2}}.
\]

Now suppose \(z(m)=\mathrm{false}\). Then
\(\noderef{BinarySequenceWeightSnoc}\) gives
\[
  \operatorname{wt}(z)=\operatorname{wt}(z').
\]
The induction hypothesis applies to \(z'\), and the false-last one-step bound
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseFalseLastBound}\) gives
\[
  F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseCount(p,m+1,z)
  \le
  2^{p(\operatorname{wt}(z')+m)-\binom{\operatorname{wt}(z')+1}{2}}
  \cdot 2^{\operatorname{wt}(z)}2^{p-\operatorname{wt}(z)}.
\]
Since the weights of \(z\) and \(z'\) are equal, the exponent recurrence
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseFalseExponentBound}\) bounds this by
\[
  2^{p(\operatorname{wt}(z)+m+1)-\binom{\operatorname{wt}(z)+1}{2}}.
\]
This completes the induction.
\end{proof}
