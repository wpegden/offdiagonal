\begin{definition}
Let \(p\) and \(k\) be natural numbers, and let
\[
  z:\{0,\ldots,k-1\}\to\{\mathrm{false},\mathrm{true}\}
\]
be a Boolean sequence. Define
\[
  \mathrm{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseCount}(p,k,z)
\]
to be the number of functions
\[
  ab:\{0,\ldots,k-1\}\to \mathbb F_2^p\times\mathbb F_2^p
\]
with the following two properties. First, \(ab\) is a bad tuple in the sense of
\(\noderef{F2BadTuple}\). Second, if \((r_0,r_1,\ldots,r_k)\) is the rank
sequence from \(\noderef{F2BadTupleRank}\), then for every zero-based index
\(i<k\),
\[
  r_{i+1}=r_i+1
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  z(i)=\mathrm{true}.
\]
Thus the Lean index \(i\) records whether the paper rank sequence increases
when the paper vector \(a_{i+1}\) is added to the first \(i\) paper vectors.
The weight \(\noderef{BinarySequenceWeight}(z)\) is the number of prescribed
rank-increase positions.
\end{definition}
