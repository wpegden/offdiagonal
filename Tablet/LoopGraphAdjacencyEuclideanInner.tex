\begin{helper}
Let \(G\) be a loop graph on a finite vertex set \(V\), and let \(T_G\) be the
Euclidean-space adjacency operator from \(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanOperator}\).
For real-valued functions \(f,g\colon V\to\mathbb R\),
\[
  \langle f,T_Gg\rangle_{\ell^2(V)}
  =
  \sum_{v\in V} f(v)(A_Gg)(v).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By the definition of \(T_G\) in \(\noderef{LoopGraphAdjacencyEuclideanOperator}\),
the vector \(T_Gg\) has \(v\)-coordinate \((A_Gg)(v)\) for every \(v\in V\).
The real Euclidean inner product on \(\ell^2(V)\) is the finite sum of pointwise
products of the two coordinate functions.  Therefore
\[
  \langle f,T_Gg\rangle_{\ell^2(V)}
  =
  \sum_{v\in V} f(v)\,(T_Gg)(v)
  =
  \sum_{v\in V} f(v)(A_Gg)(v),
\]
which is the desired identity.
\end{proof}
