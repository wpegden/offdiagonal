\begin{lemma}
Let \(F\) and \(G\) be \((n,d(F),\lambda(F))\)- and \((n,d(G),\lambda(G))\)-graphs on the same finite vertex set, and set
\[
  \eta=\max\left\{\frac{\lambda(G)^2}{d(G)^2},
  \frac{\lambda(F)\lambda(G)}{d(F)d(G)}\right\}.
\]
For the product digraph \(D(F,G)\), fix a binary shrinking sequence \(z\in\{0,1\}^t\), using the convention of \(\noderef{ProductDigraphTupleHasShrinkingSequence}\) that the one bits are the large-neighborhood cases and the zero bits are the sparse-set cases. The number of forward independent \(t\)-tuples whose shrinking sequence is \(z\) is at most
\[
  (8\eta)^{t-|z|}(d(F)n)^t.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
SKETCH:
Proceed by induction on \(t\). Given a forward independent prefix, the set \(B_i\) consists of vertices not hit by the forbidden \(G\)-neighbourhoods of earlier first coordinates, and \(A_i\) consists of vertices with at most half the expected number of \(G\)-neighbours in \(B_i\). By \(\noderef{ProductDigraphTupleHasShrinkingSequence}\), a next bit equal to \(1\) is the large-neighborhood case, so we use the trivial bound \(d(F)n\) for the next product vertex. A next bit equal to \(0\) is exactly the sparse-set case, so the next vertex must lie in \(E_F(A_i,B_i)\), and \(\noderef{SparseNeighborhoodSetBound}\) together with expander mixing for \(F\) gives at most \(8\eta d(F)n\) choices. Multiplying these stepwise bounds gives one factor \(8\eta\) for each zero bit and one factor \(1\) for each one bit, hence \((8\eta)^{t-|z|}(d(F)n)^t\).
\end{proof}
