\begin{lemma}
Let \(F\) and \(G\) be \((n,d(F),\lambda(F))\)- and \((n,d(G),\lambda(G))\)-graphs on the same finite vertex set, with \(n>0\) and \(d(F),d(G)>0\), and set
\[
  \eta=\max\left\{\frac{\lambda(G)^2}{d(G)^2},
  \frac{\lambda(F)\lambda(G)}{d(F)d(G)}\right\}.
\]
For the product digraph \(D(F,G)\), fix a binary shrinking sequence \(z\in\{0,1\}^t\), using the convention of \(\noderef{ProductDigraphTupleHasShrinkingSequence}\) that the one bits are the large-neighborhood cases and the zero bits are the sparse-set cases. The number of forward independent \(t\)-tuples whose shrinking sequence is \(z\) is at most
\[
  (8\eta)^{t-|z|}(d(F)n)^t.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Let \(D=D(F,G)\), and for a binary string \(z\in\{0,1\}^t\) write
\[
  C(t,z)
\]
for the fixed-sequence tuple count in \(\noderef{ProductDigraphFixedSequenceTupleCount}\). Thus \(C(t,z)\) counts exactly the forward independent \(t\)-tuples of vertices of \(D\) whose shrinking sequence is \(z\), in the sense of \(\noderef{ProductDigraphTupleHasShrinkingSequence}\). We prove
\[
  C(t,z)\le (8\eta)^{t-|z|}(d(F)n)^t
\]
by induction on \(t\), uniformly in the binary string \(z\).

For \(t=0\), there is exactly one empty tuple. The empty binary string has weight \(0\) by \(\noderef{BinarySequenceWeight}\), so the desired bound reads
\[
  1\le (8\eta)^0(d(F)n)^0=1.
\]
This proves the base case.

Now assume \(t\ge 1\), and let \(z\in\{0,1\}^t\). Let \(z'\in\{0,1\}^{t-1}\) be the prefix obtained by deleting the last bit. Any \(t\)-tuple
\[
  \mathbf v=(v_1,\ldots,v_t)
\]
counted by \(C(t,z)\) has a prefix
\[
  \mathbf v'=(v_1,\ldots,v_{t-1})
\]
counted by \(C(t-1,z')\). Indeed, \(\noderef{ProductDigraphTupleHasShrinkingSequence}\) says that the shrinking bit at each index is determined only by the earlier first coordinates, and the forward-independence condition for the prefix is inherited from the forward-independence condition for the full tuple. Therefore the prefix has exactly the shrinking sequence \(z'\).

Fix such a prefix \(\mathbf v'\), and write
\[
  v_i=(a_i,b_i)
\]
for its product vertices. Define
\[
  B_t=V\setminus\bigcup_{i<t}N_G(a_i),
  \qquad
  A_t=\left\{u\in V:\ |N_G(u)\cap B_t|
      \le \frac{d(G)|B_t|}{2n}\right\}.
\]
These two sets are determined entirely by the prefix \(\mathbf v'\). We now bound the number of possible last vertices \(v_t=(a_t,b_t)\).

First suppose the last bit of \(z\) is \(0\). By the convention in \(\noderef{ProductDigraphTupleHasShrinkingSequence}\), the zero bit is exactly the sparse-set case, so \(a_t\in A_t\). Also, because \(v_t\) is a vertex of the product digraph, \(\noderef{ProductDigraph}\) and \(\noderef{ProductDigraphVertex}\) give
\[
  a_t b_t\in E(F).
\]
Finally, since the full tuple is forward independent in \(D\), no earlier vertex \(v_i\) has an arc to \(v_t\). By the arc definition of \(D(F,G)\) in \(\noderef{ProductDigraph}\), this means \(a_i b_t\notin E(G)\) for every \(i<t\), and hence \(b_t\in B_t\). Thus every possible last vertex in the zero-bit case is counted by
\[
  e_F(A_t,B_t),
\]
the ordered edge count from \(A_t\) to \(B_t\) in \(F\), as defined in \(\noderef{LoopGraphEdgeCountBetween}\).

Apply \(\noderef{SparseNeighborhoodSetBound}\) to the graph \(G\) and the set \(B_t\). The definition of \(A_t\) is exactly the sparse-neighborhood hypothesis in that lemma, so
\[
  |A_t||B_t|\le \frac{4\lambda(G)^2}{d(G)^2}n^2. \tag{1}
\]
Apply \(\noderef{ExpanderMixingLemma}\) to \(F\) with the sets \(A_t\) and \(B_t\). Since \(e_F(A_t,B_t)\) is the ordered count from \(\noderef{LoopGraphEdgeCountBetween}\), the mixing lemma gives
\[
  e_F(A_t,B_t)
  \le \frac{d(F)}{n}|A_t||B_t|
      +\lambda(F)\sqrt{|A_t||B_t|}.
\]
We spell out the numerical estimate. From (1) and \(n,d(G)>0\),
\[
  \frac{d(F)}{n}|A_t||B_t|
  \le 4n\,\frac{d(F)\lambda(G)^2}{d(G)^2}.
\]
Also, since the \(\lambda\)-parameters in an \((n,d,\lambda)\)-graph are nonnegative spectral bounds by \(\noderef{LoopGraphNdLambda}\), (1) gives
\[
  \sqrt{|A_t||B_t|}\le \frac{2\lambda(G)n}{d(G)}.
\]
Hence
\[
  \lambda(F)\sqrt{|A_t||B_t|}
  \le 2n\,\frac{\lambda(F)\lambda(G)}{d(G)}
  \le 4n\,\frac{\lambda(F)\lambda(G)}{d(G)}.
\]
Combining these two estimates with the previous mixing bound gives
\[
  e_F(A_t,B_t)
  \le 4n\left(\frac{d(F)\lambda(G)^2}{d(G)^2}
        +\frac{\lambda(F)\lambda(G)}{d(G)}\right)
\]
Finally, by the definition of \(\eta\) and \(d(F)>0\),
\[
  \frac{\lambda(G)^2}{d(G)^2}\le \eta,
  \qquad
  \frac{\lambda(F)\lambda(G)}{d(F)d(G)}\le \eta.
\]
Multiplying the second inequality by \(d(F)\) and adding the two displayed bounds yields
\[
  e_F(A_t,B_t)\le 8\eta\, d(F)n. \tag{2}
\]
Thus, for each fixed prefix in the zero-bit case, there are at most \(8\eta\,d(F)n\) choices for \(v_t\).

By the induction hypothesis, the number of possible prefixes is at most
\[
  (8\eta)^{(t-1)-|z'|}(d(F)n)^{t-1}.
\]
Multiplying by the zero-bit choice bound (2) gives
\[
  (8\eta)^{(t-1)-|z'|}(d(F)n)^{t-1}
  \cdot 8\eta\,d(F)n.
\]
Since the last bit is \(0\), \(\noderef{BinarySequenceWeight}\) gives \(|z|=|z'|\). Hence the last displayed expression is
\[
  (8\eta)^{t-|z|}(d(F)n)^t,
\]
which is the desired bound in the zero-bit case.

It remains to consider the case where the last bit of \(z\) is \(1\). In this case \(\noderef{ProductDigraphTupleHasShrinkingSequence}\) places the last first-coordinate \(a_t\) in the large-neighborhood case. We do not use any sparse estimate; we only use the trivial bound on the number of possible product vertices. By \(\noderef{ProductDigraphVertex}\), a vertex of \(D(F,G)\) is an ordered pair \((a,b)\) with \(ab\in E(F)\). Since \(F\) is an \((n,d(F),\lambda(F))\)-graph, \(\noderef{LoopGraphNdLambda}\) and \(\noderef{LoopGraphDegree}\) say that there are \(n\) choices for \(a\) and, for each \(a\), exactly \(d(F)\) choices for \(b\) with \(ab\in E(F)\). Therefore
\[
  |V(D)|=d(F)n,
\]
so for each fixed prefix there are at most \(d(F)n\) choices for \(v_t\).

The induction hypothesis again gives at most
\[
  (8\eta)^{(t-1)-|z'|}(d(F)n)^{t-1}
\]
prefixes. Multiplying by the trivial \(d(F)n\) choices for the final vertex gives
\[
  (8\eta)^{(t-1)-|z'|}(d(F)n)^t.
\]
Since the last bit is \(1\), \(\noderef{BinarySequenceWeight}\) gives \(|z|=|z'|+1\), so
\[
  (t-1)-|z'|=t-|z|.
\]
Thus the last displayed expression is exactly
\[
  (8\eta)^{t-|z|}(d(F)n)^t.
\]

The induction is complete, and therefore the displayed bound holds for every fixed binary shrinking sequence \(z\).
\end{proof}
