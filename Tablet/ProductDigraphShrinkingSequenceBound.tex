\begin{lemma}
In the product-digraph counting argument, fix a binary shrinking sequence \(z\in\{0,1\}^t\). The number of forward independent \(t\)-tuples whose shrinking sequence is \(z\) is at most
\[
  (8\eta)^{t-|z|}(d(F)n)^t.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
SKETCH:
Proceed by induction on \(t\). Given a forward independent prefix, the set \(B_i\) consists of vertices not hit by the forbidden \(G\)-neighbourhoods of earlier first coordinates, and \(A_i\) consists of vertices with at most half the expected number of \(G\)-neighbours in \(B_i\). If the next bit is \(1\), use the trivial bound \(d(F)n\) for the next product vertex. If the next bit is \(0\), the next vertex must lie in \(E_F(A_i,B_i)\), and \noderef{SparseNeighborhoodSetBound} together with expander mixing for \(F\) gives at most \(8\eta d(F)n\) choices. Multiplying these stepwise bounds gives the claimed estimate.
\end{proof}
