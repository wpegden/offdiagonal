\begin{helper}
Let \(\varepsilon,\lambda\in\mathbb R\), let \(s,a,t\in\mathbb N\), and
assume \(1\le t\le s-1\). Put \(j=s-t\). Suppose
\[
  \binom{s+a}{t}\le 2^{(a+j)\lambda},
\]
\[
  a\lambda\le \frac{\varepsilon s}{4},
\]
and
\[
  j\lambda+\frac{3j}{2}-\frac{j^2}{2}\le \frac{\varepsilon s}{4}.
\]
Then
\[
  \binom{s+a}{t}
    2^{(s-1)(t+s+a)-\binom{t+1}{2}}
  \le
  2^{s^2/2+s(s+a)-3s/2-(s+a)+\varepsilon s/2}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(1\le t\le s-1\) and \(j=s-t\), we have \(1\le j\le s-1\) and
\(s-j=t\). Applying \(\noderef{F2NearDiagonalExponentIdentity}\) with
\(k=s+a\) gives
\[
  (s-1)(t+s+a)-\binom{t+1}{2}
  =
  s^2/2+s(s+a)-3s/2-(s+a)+3j/2-j^2/2.
\]
Using the assumed binomial bound and multiplying by the nonnegative power of
\(2\), the left hand side in the conclusion is at most
\[
  2^{(a+j)\lambda}\,
  2^{s^2/2+s(s+a)-3s/2-(s+a)+3j/2-j^2/2}.
\]
Combining powers of \(2\), this is
\[
  2^{
    s^2/2+s(s+a)-3s/2-(s+a)
    +a\lambda+
    \left(j\lambda+3j/2-j^2/2\right)}.
\]
The two control assumptions bound the last two displayed summands by
\(\varepsilon s/4\) each. Hence the exponent is at most
\[
  s^2/2+s(s+a)-3s/2-(s+a)+\varepsilon s/2.
\]
Monotonicity of \(2^x\) proves the claimed summand bound.
\end{proof}
