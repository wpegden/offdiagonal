\begin{helper}
Let \(p,m\) be natural numbers, let
\[
  ab:\{0,\ldots,m\}\to \mathbb F_2^p\times\mathbb F_2^p,
\]
and let \(z:\{0,\ldots,m\}\to\{\mathrm{false},\mathrm{true}\}\) be a Boolean
rank-increase pattern as in \(\noderef{F2BadTupleFixedIncreaseCount}\).
Suppose that \(ab\) is bad and that, for every zero-based \(i\le m\), the rank
sequence of \(ab\) increases from \(r_i\) to \(r_{i+1}\) if and only if
\(z(i)=\mathrm{true}\). Let \(ab'\) be the prefix tuple of length \(m\), and
let \(z'\) be the restricted Boolean sequence \(z'(i)=z(i)\) for
zero-based \(i<m\). Then \(ab'\) is bad, and for every zero-based \(i<m\), the
rank sequence of \(ab'\) increases from \(r'_i\) to \(r'_{i+1}\) if and only if
\(z'(i)=\mathrm{true}\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The badness of \(ab'\) follows immediately from
\(\noderef{F2BadTuplePrefixRestriction}\). It remains to verify the
rank-increase pattern. Fix a zero-based index \(i<m\). Since \(i<m\), both
\(i\le m\) and \(i+1\le m\). Applying
\(\noderef{F2BadTupleRankPrefixRestriction}\) at the two prefix lengths \(i\)
and \(i+1\) shows that
\[
  r'_i=r_i
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  r'_{i+1}=r_{i+1}.
\]
Therefore the statement \(r'_{i+1}=r'_i+1\) is exactly the same statement as
\(r_{i+1}=r_i+1\) for the original tuple. By the assumed fixed-increase
condition on \(ab\), this original statement is equivalent to
\(z(i)=\mathrm{true}\). The restricted sequence satisfies \(z'(i)=z(i)\), so
the prefix rank increases at \(i\) if and only if \(z'(i)=\mathrm{true}\), as
claimed.
\end{proof}
