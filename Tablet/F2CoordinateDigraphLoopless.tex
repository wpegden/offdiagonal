\begin{helper}
Let \(W\) be a type, let \(p\) be a natural number, and let
\[
  x,y:W\to \mathbb F_2^p
\]
be coordinate maps satisfying \(\langle x(w),y(w)\rangle=1\) for every \(w\in W\).
Define a digraph \(D\) on \(W\) by
\[
  D(a,b)\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \langle x(a),y(b)\rangle=0.
\]
Then \(D\) is loopless.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
We prove the loopless condition from \(\noderef{DigraphLoopless}\). Fix a vertex
\(w\in W\). If \(D(w,w)\) held, then by the definition of \(D\) we would have
\(\langle x(w),y(w)\rangle=0\). The diagonal hypothesis gives
\(\langle x(w),y(w)\rangle=1\). Hence \(1=0\) in \(\mathbb F_2\), impossible.
Therefore \(D(w,w)\) is false for every \(w\), so \(D\) is loopless.
\end{proof}
